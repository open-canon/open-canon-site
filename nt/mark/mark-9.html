St Mark — Chapter 9

Jesus is transfigured on the mountain—He casts out an unclean spirit—He teaches concerning His death and resurrection, who will be greatest, and the condemnation of those who offend His little ones.

1 And he said unto them, Verily I say unto you, That there be some of them that stand here, which shall not taste of death, till they have seen the kingdom of God come with power.

5 And Peter answered and said to Jesus, Master, it is good for us to be here: and let us make three tabernacles; one for thee, and one for Moses, and one for Elias.

27 But Jesus took him by the hand, and alifted him up; and he arose.
28 And when he was come into the house, his disciples asked him privately, Why could not we cast him out?
29 And he said unto them, This kind can come forth by nothing, but by prayer and afasting.
30 ¶ And they departed thence, and passed through Galilee; and he would not that any man should know it.

32 But they understood not that saying, and were afraid to ask him.

34 But they held their peace: for by the way they had adisputed among themselves, who should be the bgreatest.
